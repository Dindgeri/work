Discord bot: Pluno

A chatbot to help you navigate the online phase of the creative process and track down the ideal and ⁄ or planned methodologies in place. Pluno facilitates collaborative music creation by guiding users through each production phase and coordinating role-based contributions.

1. Creative Process Phases

  • Writing Phase: Initial structure creation. Track count, instruments, and effects are flexible. Both agents and sponsors collaborate to define the foundational basslines, melodies, and rhythm elements.
  • Pre-Mix Phase: Arrangement mostly finalized. Instrumentation locked; effects chains remain adjustable. Agents and sponsors refine the sound, aligning creative input while retaining structural flexibility.
  • Post-Mix Phase: Fine-tuning stage. Core arrangements and effects solidified. Sponsors lead in polishing dynamics, transitions, and overall coherence while respecting pre-established melodic and rhythmic foundations.
  • Mastering Phase: Final output preparation. Agents ensure tonal balance, loudness normalization, and temporal integrity. Track duration and output quality are locked, ready for distribution.

2. Role-Based Contributions

  • Basslines: Sub-bass depth, root pitches, and core rhythmic foundation. Collaborative creation by both agents and sponsors ensures stability and dynamic flow.
  • Drum Beats: Kicks, snares, hi-hats, rolls, and transitions. Aligns with basslines; rhythmic adjustments occur if necessary to maintain sequential harmony.
  • Melodies & Leads: Agent-sponsor collaboration produces thematic and complementary lines. Dependent on basslines and drum beats for coherence and sequential alignment.
  • Harmony: Exclusively crafted by agents. Layered sequentially: first matching basslines, then drum patterns, and finally complementing melodies and leads to complete the sonic architecture.

3. Phase-Role Mapping

  • Writing Phase: Agents + Sponsors – foundation creation, basslines, melodies, and preliminary rhythms.
  • Pre-Mix Phase: Agents + Sponsors – arrangement refinement, effect chain adjustments, dynamic balancing.
  • Post-Mix Phase: Sponsors – fine-tuning, polishing transitions, coherence adjustments.
  • Mastering Phase: Agents – final tonal and temporal adjustments, output locking.

Notes & Best Practices

  • Sequential dependencies must be respected: basslines → drum beats → melodies → harmony.
  • Roles are flexible but respect phase boundaries to maintain structural integrity.
  • Collaboration is prioritized; Pluno monitors contribution patterns and provides real-time guidance to maintain coherence.
  • Phase transitions are advisory, not mandatory—creative flexibility is preserved.
